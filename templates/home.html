Welcome to the Interactive Calculus Review!

This site is designed to help you practice your differentiation skills. The problems are parsed directly from a LaTeX document and presented in an interactive format.

How to Use This Site

  • Navigate to the practice page to see all the problems, grouped by category.
  • Use the filters at the top to focus on specific topics.
  • Mark as Done: Use the checkbox on the right of each problem to mark it as completed. Your progress is saved in your browser!
  • Check Your Answer: Type your solution into the text box and click "Check Answer" to see if you're right.

How to Type Solutions

The answer checker is smart, but not a genius. It normalizes your input to match the LaTeX format. Here are the rules:

  • For powers, use the ^ symbol. Example: x^2
  • For fractions, use parentheses if needed. The checker isn't very smart about this, so be explicit.
  • Trig and log functions are recognized automatically. Just type sin(x), cos(x), ln(x), etc.
  • Example: To type \( \sin^2 x \), you would write sin^2 x.
  • Disclaimer: The checker compares your formatted text to the solution's text. It does not check for mathematical equivalence (e.g., it won't know that (x+1)^2 is the same as x^2+2x+1). You must match the form of the provided solution.
